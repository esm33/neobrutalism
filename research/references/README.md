## Common Neo-Brutalist Patterns Observed

### Color Schemes
- **Primary**: Yellow, cyan, magenta, purple
- **Base**: Black and white high contrast
- **Accents**: Bright, saturated colors
- **No**: Gradients, subtle tones, pastels (unless deliberately bright)

### Typography
- Large, bold headings
- System fonts or simple sans-serif
- All caps for emphasis
- Tight line-height
- High contrast in sizes

### Layout
- Thick black borders (3-5px)
- Card-based compartmentalization
- Hard drop shadows (offset, not blurred)
- 90° angles (no rounded corners, or deliberately rounded for contrast)
- Exposed grid structure

### Components
- Buttons: Thick borders, hard shadows, bright colors
- Forms: Clear borders, functional styling
- Cards: Boxed sections with heavy borders
- Windows/Dialogs: Computer-window aesthetic

### Overall Aesthetic
- Bold and unapologetic
- Functional first, decorative second
- High energy through color and scale
- Raw, honest structure
- Anti-corporate, deliberately rough

## How to Use These References

1. **Study each image for 2-3 minutes**
   - What makes it feel neo-brutalist?
   - What specific techniques are used?
   - How does it balance function and aesthetics?

2. **Identify patterns across images**
   - Which colors appear repeatedly?
   - What border/shadow styles are common?
   - How is hierarchy established?

3. **Apply to your project**
   - Use as inspiration, not direct copying
   - Adapt techniques to your content
   - Maintain the "raw, honest, bold" philosophy

4. **Compare your work**
   - Does your design feel as bold?
   - Are borders thick enough?
   - Is contrast high enough?
   - Would a corporate brand reject it? (Good sign!)
